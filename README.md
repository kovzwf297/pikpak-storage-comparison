## Why MEGA's Transfer Quota Is a Real Problem

Let's be honest about what MEGA is: a solid encrypted storage service that's been around since 2013, has 300 million users, and gives you a genuinely useful 20GB free plan. The end-to-end encryption is real. The speeds in testing are competitive.

But the quota system creates friction in specific ways that matter:

- **Free users** hit the bandwidth limit after downloading roughly 5–10GB in a 6-hour window. Download a shared folder? You might exhaust your quota before finishing.
- **Paid users** (PRO I at €9.99/month) get 2TB of monthly transfer quota. Move a lot of large files and that goes faster than you'd expect.
- **Shared links** consume the *owner's* quota, not just the downloader's. Share a popular file? You'll drain your own bandwidth allowance as other people download it.
- **Workarounds** exist — VPN IP switching, JDownloader, MegaBasterd — but they're all inelegant and some technically violate MEGA's terms of service.

If your use case is casual document backup, MEGA's quota system is a non-issue. If you're regularly moving, storing, or sharing large media files, it becomes a persistent annoyance.
